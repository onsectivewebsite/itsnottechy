# Case Study Outreach

Real case studies with named clients are the highest-value citation content a services business can publish. This folder contains ready-to-send outreach and the permission paperwork to get there.

## Recommended workflow

1. **Shortlist 5–8 candidate clients** where the results are strong and the engagement was positive. Good candidates: NPS 9 or 10, measurable outcomes attributable to the engagement, a champion at the client who would be willing to speak on record.
2. **Send the permission-request email** (`01-permission-request.md`) — one-to-one, from a senior practitioner, never in bulk.
3. **Collect signed permission via the consent form** (`02-consent-form.md`). The form covers named attribution, metric disclosure, and a 30-day review window before publication.
4. **Interview the client for 30 minutes** using the discovery guide (`03-interview-guide.md`). Record with permission.
5. **Draft the case study** using the template (`04-case-study-template.md`).
6. **Client reviews the draft**, approves via email.
7. **Publish** in `src/data/caseStudies.ts` with `status: "named"`.

## How many case studies you actually need

Three to five named case studies is enough for:
- AI citation visibility in "[your category] case study" and "[your category] client results" queries
- Clutch, G2, and GoodFirms review profiles (each platform wants verifiable client references)
- Sales team enablement — the single highest-ROI use of case study content

Going beyond five produces diminishing returns unless each additional one unlocks a new vertical or use case the others don't cover.
