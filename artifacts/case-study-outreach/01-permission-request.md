# Permission request email — send to client champion

**Subject:** Would [Client Company] be up for a public case study?

---

Hi [First Name],

The work we shipped together over the last [duration] drove some of the strongest outcomes I've seen on our team this year — [specific outcome with number, e.g., "the 412% organic traffic lift between Q1 and Q4"]. With your permission, I'd love to publish a named case study telling that story.

Here's what I'm proposing:

- **Scope:** A full-length case study on [itsnottechy.com/case-studies](https://itsnottechy.com/case-studies), with [Client Company]'s name, specific metrics we're comfortable sharing publicly, and the approach we took.
- **What I'd need from you:** A 30-minute interview (I'll send questions in advance), a one-time review of the draft before we publish, and a pull quote with your name and title.
- **What you'd get:** Public reference to your team's results; I'll also send you a branded PDF version for your own sales and investor materials.
- **What we won't publish:** Anything you flag as confidential. You have final approval over every number, quote, and claim before we go live.

If you're open to it but need to loop in legal or comms, I can share a permission form that covers standard terms. Or we can just handle it via email — whatever's easiest on your side.

If it's not the right time, or not something your team does, totally understood — a quick "no" is fine and we'll keep the engagement private.

Any interest?

Thanks,
[Your name]
[Your role]
[Calendar link or contact info]

---

## Customization notes

- Replace [bracketed] fields before sending.
- Keep the "quick no is fine" out — clients who feel pressured don't agree, and they stop answering future emails.
- Send from the most senior practitioner on the account, not marketing.
- Follow up once after 10 business days if no response. After a second no-reply, let it go.
